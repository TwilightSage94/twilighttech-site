What makes it different from the AI you've already tried

Trust-gated actions Every operation has a permission tier: autonomous, notify, confirm, authenticate, prohibited. You decide — per action, per context — what J-Bot can do without checking with you. Dangerous things always require explicit approval.
Cross-domain context A support email automatically informs ticket priority. A calendar conflict influences how an email gets drafted. The agent doesn't lose track when you switch topics — it maintains state across the whole conversation.
Production-grade 200+ tool handlers, 14 internal subsystems, 4,300+ automated tests, multi-provider LLM with cost tracking and audit logging. It runs in production every day.(Spoiler: it's running this site for me.)

FAQ

Things people actually ask

If you have a question that isn't here, the audit call is free until I'm convinced we're not a fit. Ask anything.

What if I'm not sure I need a Build after the Audit?
That's the whole point of the audit being separate. You walk out with a written report you can act on (or not). About a third of audit deliverables include "you don't actually need a Build, here's a $50/month tool that solves 80% of this" — and I tell you that honestly. The audit fee credits toward a Build if you do want one, but it's not a contract.
Can I just hire you hourly for an existing problem?
Not at this stage — I'm too small to support hourly engagements without losing the deeper builds. But the Custom tier covers most "I have one specific thing" cases, and we can scope it as a fixed-price mini-engagement. Email me with the problem, I'll tell you within 24 hours if it fits.
What if my data can't leave my network?
J-Bot supports a fully air-gapped deployment using local LLMs (Ollama on your hardware). Slower than cloud and capped at smaller models, but zero data leaves your network. Most regulated clients use a hybrid: local model for sensitive operations, cloud for everything else, all governed by trust gates you control.
How long until I see ROI?
Realistic answer: 4-8 weeks after Build. Week 1-2 is your team learning to delegate to it. Week 3-4 is the agent learning your patterns. By month 2 the time savings are measurable. I include a baseline measurement at the start of the Build and a re-measurement at day 30 so we're both looking at the same numbers.
What happens if a model breaks or the API changes?
J-Bot is multi-provider by design — it fails over between NVIDIA NIM, Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, and local Ollama automatically. Provider outages don't take you down. API breaking changes are my problem, not yours; I update the platform and your deployment gets the patch.
Do you sign NDAs?
Yes, before the audit if you need one. I have a mutual NDA template ready. The audit recording stays with you; I keep my notes for the duration of the engagement and delete them on request after.
Who owns the code and configuration after the Build?
You do. The custom domain pack I build for your business is yours. The base J-Bot platform is open under the Twilight Tech license, and I keep maintaining it for everyone — but your config, your prompts, your integrations, your data: yours. You can fire me and another engineer can take it over, and they'd have the source.
What size businesses fit best?
5-50 employees is the sweet spot. 50-200 is workable but the integration scope grows. Solo operators (1-4 people) often get more value from the local laptop deployment than a full Build — the audit tells us which path fits.
Is this a SaaS subscription or do I install something?
Neither, in the traditional sense. J-Bot is a platform you run — Windows or Linux service, your hardware (or a small VPS I help you provision). No SaaS fees from me. You pay for the Build, optionally a Retainer, and the underlying LLM provider you choose (typically $5-50/month at SMB scale).
